// Tinkering through life.

My journey of playing with the notion of "what if I do this..?" with things started pretty early from what I heard from my parents. One of my favourite toys had broken when I was a kid, it was a RC car. This led me to dig into it with a spare screwdriver and see and learn how it worked with assistance from my dad. This helped me learn what a motor does, what a battery is and that it operated on DC current which also led me to be curious about the link between this DC current and a cassete tape of AC/DC's album my dad had which then got me into music thereby leading to a chain reaction of events that made me the "me" I am today.

-- Tinkering domain : Electronics and Software.

My primary tinkering domain is easily electronics interestingly because growing up in the post Y2K era, there was a ton of electronic waste to play around with as a kid. Everything from LEDs to filament bulbs to lasers salvaged from DVD players to microwave oven transformers that can take someones heartbeat away in a heartbeat.

Once my prefrontal cortex started developing as a teen, I got into the field of controlling these electronics with code. This made me dive into the world of software and I ended up really really loving it as a perfect bridge between the abstract logic of code and physical hardware.

-- Spending time off-screen : Reclamation of leisure and attention resistance.

In the modern world that weaponizes attention span to synthetically engrain a new product we don't want into our minds, it is a saddening thought to realize that forming a reasonable unbiased thought is becoming rarer and rarer.

To resist this blitzkreig of digital blackholes sucking me into it, I resort to choosing a life that we forgot about, a low tech life. Ofcourse doing this in absolution is impossible since most things you need to function run on the digital realm. So I started going out on walks when I fully disconnect for a couple hours a day where I walk somewhere and either read a book or just observe the surroundings and channel my thoughts.

-- Wishlist for expanding my space.

One thing I value a lot about the art of tinkering is the fact that it allows one to be independent of the wave of consumerism that rose in the last few decades and just fix something that broke instead of getting a new thing to replace it.

To do this one of the easiest and the most impactful thing a tinkerer can have is a good 3D printer and thus this would easily be one of the things I would like to add to my personal arseanal of tools. Ofcourse this also must be easily repairable and DIY friendly.
