--Introduction.

This session also involved talks on the affordance as a concept and the use of it in the setting of tinkering using the kits used in session 2 as a base. We also worked on building our own animatronic robotic system using the tools available around us as a concrete method to learn about the concept of affordance in practice.

--Reflection Questions.

  1. First your main observations during the tinkering sessions. Can you recognise most of the aspects of the framework in action?

    During the sessions, we played around with various tinkering tools such as the makedo kit mentioned in the building block section above, raspberry pi kits and all. For each of the different kits, we had no idea what it did or should do initially and it was discovered my purely tinkering, trial-and-error and brainstorming.

  2. Check up on the concept of Affordance. What is the application to the materials used in tinkering? To what extend are the original definition (Gibson) or the HCI oriented re-definition of Norman useful?

    When we talk about the word "Affordance", we think of monetary affordance of commodities. In the realm of tinkering based on the works provided and the lecture takeaways, it is clear that "Affordance" in this context refers to what the environment offers an individual. This can refer to the various tools and the subsequent solution space that gets unlocked using these tools. The way the user decides to use these tools to explore the affordance is the key process in tinkering.

  3. Read the chapter 'Designing for tinkerability' from Design Make Play. Which criteria for tinkerability are described? Use these for the following table:

    In the chapter, the criterias discussed are "Process over product", "Setting of themes and not challenges", "Highlighting diverse examples", "Tinkering with space", "Encourage engagement with people", "Posing open questions over gving answers" and "Combining diving in and stepping back".
