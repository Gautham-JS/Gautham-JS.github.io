// Session 1 : Getting Started.

--Introduction.

In this session the first requirement was creating ones own portfolio website, that is this very webpage we have before our eyes :D Next, we dive into the design process of tinkering tools.

For this I decided to start off with an initial baby step of prototyping one of the biggest hurdles in software in my opinion, having a humane vision of abstraction. For this I had a vision to build a set of jigsaw and lego inspired card set that can be interfaced to visually represent algorithms and how various sub-components piece together to create a larger component. We will refer to this hypothetical tool as "Algocards"

--Design Prototyping Algocards.

For this design, I initially consided the design of the cards itself as drawings, an initial drawing-board of ideas on how the cards can represent the most common paradigm of software components, an input and output method. For this, 2 appraoches were considered:
  1. Jigsaw blocks:

    I thought a jigsaw inspired card would be a good starting point as the concave notches can be an input to whatever a card represents and a convex notch can be the output.

  2. Thread connected blocks:

    Another contender for this would be the use of threads where the information flows from left to right when the cards are laid out on the table. Similar to how most people comprehend written language.

--Reflection Questions.

  1. Tinkering is widely associated with trial-and-error and brainstorming. Find dictionary definitions of the three techniques, and compare them. Does the definition of tinkering match the definition we have in the course? Also, ask a generative AI tool for the definitions and differences. Which aspects does the answer provided miss?

    According to Cambridge Dictionary, The definitions of the three techniques are:

    • Tinkering : To make small changes to something, especially in an attempt to repair or improve it
    • Train and Error : A way of achieving an aim or solving a problem by trying a number of different methods and learning from the mistakes that you make
    • Brainstorming : An activity or business method in which a group of people meet to suggest a lot of new ideas for possible development

    Asking the same definitions to a generative AI, Claude AI in this case yields these:
    • Tinkering : Tinkering is a hands-on approach where you explore, experiment, and modify something to understand how it works or to improve it.
    • Train and Error : Trial-and-error is a problem-solving method where multiple attempts are made until a solution is found.
    • Brainstorming : Brainstorming is a creative thinking technique focused on generating multiple ideas before evaluating them.
